> The internship survey/report below follows the evaluation questions about internship experience, improvement suggestions, and expectations for future participation in the program.

### Additional Questions

**1. What were you most satisfied with during the internship?**  

What I was most satisfied with was the mentor's guidance method and the practical working spirit here. Instead of simply giving ready-made answers, the mentor created an open mechanism for me to research, experiment, and directly troubleshoot real technical issues myself, such as ALB connection refusal errors, S3/CloudFront OAC permission issues, and multi-stage Dockerfile optimization. The feeling of successfully configuring a complete secure network infrastructure system on AWS and seeing the Sport Shop MVP web project connect safely to MongoDB Atlas and run smoothly on the Internet gave me strong professional confidence.

**2. What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  

Because the amount of Cloud knowledge, especially AWS systems such as VPC, ECS Fargate, ALB, and ECR, is very large and highly complex for interns who are new to the field, I think the company could provide a roadmap or concise best-practice documents from the first week. This would help future interns identify key network infrastructure checkpoints more easily and develop a broader system mindset before starting real implementation projects.

**3. If recommending this internship to friends, would you advise them to intern here? Why?**  

Definitely YES. The environment here is not limited to theory or simulated exercises; it is a great opportunity to get hands-on experience with the real work of a DevOps/Cloud Engineer. Interns can learn how to package applications through containerization, design production-ready secure networks, optimize cloud costs, and train the most important skill: reading logs and diagnosing system issues when errors occur. These are valuable experiences that very few places allow interns to directly practice.

### Suggestions & Expectations

**1. Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  

I suggest that the company could organize a few short internal knowledge-sharing sessions between weeks so everyone can discuss common system errors together. The company could also add several team bonding activities so interns have more chances to exchange ideas and share learning experiences with one another.

**2. Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  

I would really like to have the opportunity to continue accompanying and staying connected with the program or the company in the future. The practical lessons I gained have significantly strengthened my infrastructure mindset, and I am ready to challenge myself with deeper areas such as CI/CD automation, advanced security firewall configuration with AWS WAF, and building a monitoring system.

**3. Other comments:**  

I would like to sincerely thank Mentor Luân and the FCAJ support team for always accompanying me, providing enthusiastic guidance, and even helping me resolve system issues outside working hours. This internship has truly been a wonderful launchpad for my career journey in Cloud Computing.
