# Summary Report: FCAJ Community Day – 23/05/2026

### Event Objectives

The event aimed to share the latest technology trends, especially AI applications in software development and cloud, while creating networking opportunities for members of the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) community.

### Speakers

- **Nguyen Gia Hung** – Solutions Architect, AWS Vietnam & Founder of FCAJ
- **Tinh Truong** – Platform Engineer, GoTymeX
- **Phan Nguyễn Hải Anh** – AWS Community Builder, G-AsiaPacific Vietnam
- **Nguyễn Tuấn Thịnh** – DevOps Engineer
- **Uyển and Thảo** – Team VIB – LotusHacks Journey Sharing
- **Duc Dao** – Solution Architect, Cloud Kinetics
- **Vy Lam** – Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank

### Key Highlights

#### The future of jobs and the paradox of cheapness in the AI era (Nguyen Gia Hung)

Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung presented an insightful perspective on an interesting paradox: as AI makes software development cheaper and faster, the demand for software applications increases rapidly. He likened this to how the adoption of LED bulbs led people to consume more light overall, rather than saving energy.

Consequently, the number of software projects, applications, and AI products will skyrocket. This creates numerous new job opportunities, particularly in operations, scaling systems, product maintenance, and integrating AI into enterprises. He emphasized that in the future, large companies will not just need developers who write code, but also individuals who know how to operate and optimize large-scale AI systems.

#### Context Engineering (Tinh Truong)

Mr. Tinh Truong presented on the topic "Context Is Everything." He argued that while current AI models are extremely powerful, they fail primarily due to a lack of good context. Common mistakes include:
- Cramming too much irrelevant information (Internet Puller Problem)
- Repeating what the AI already knows
- Failing to specify Goals, Constraints, and Success Criteria

He introduced how to build a Second AI Brain—a system that helps the AI remember personal knowledge, projects, and long-term goals, resulting in more accurate and personalized responses.

#### Tools and solutions discussed

- **Amazon Quick**: Build smart AI Assistants to automate Minutes of Meeting creation, send emails, and analyze data (Phan Nguyễn Hải Anh).  
- **Amazon CloudFront**: Utilize Edge Locations as a foundation for modern architectures (Nguyễn Tuấn Thịnh).  
- **Multi-Agent System**: Build enterprise-grade multi-agent AI systems (Vy Lam).  
- **LLM Deep Dive**: Gain a deep understanding of the inner workings of Large Language Models (Duc Dao).  
- **UTMorpho**: A real-world project built in 36 hours at LotusHacks (Team VIB).  

### Key Takeaways

#### Career direction and the IT labor market

- The Vietnamese IT market is shifting rapidly from outsourcing to AI products and solutions.  
- Today's most crucial skills are not just coding, but also Context Engineering, building real products, and understanding the business domain.  
- Students should start building their portfolios with personal projects (Second Brain, AI Assistant, Multi-Agent System...) while still in school.  

#### Technical skills

- How to provide high-quality Context to AI for optimal results.  
- Deepen understanding of how LLMs operate and how to construct Multi-Agent systems.  
- Apply AWS services (Quick, CloudFront, Bedrock) to practical projects.  
- Gain experience participating in hackathons and building products under tight deadlines.  

### Applying to Work / Internship

- Apply Second Brain to manage personal knowledge and project notes.  
- Build a Friendly AI Assistant to support daily work (meeting notes, summarization, scheduling).  
- Use Context Engineering techniques when working with AI models (ChatGPT, Claude, Amazon Bedrock, etc.).  
- Integrate CloudFront into web projects to optimize speed and security.  
- Experiment with building Multi-Agent Systems for complex problem solving.  
- Leverage LLM understanding to optimize prompts and reduce token costs.  

These skills will help me feel more confident when applying for internships or full-time roles as a Cloud/AI Engineer, Solution Architect, or DevOps Engineer.

### Event Experience

I was able to hear directly from professionals working in major companies and the community. The content was highly practical and closely related to real work, rather than just theoretical. The live demos on Amazon Quick and Second Brain particularly helped me visualize how to apply these concepts immediately.

#### Lessons learned

- AI is a powerful tool, but humans remain the deciding factor through providing context.  
- Building production-grade products is more important than just learning theory.  
- Participating in the community and events like FCAJ is the fastest and most effective way to learn.  
- Always maintain a continuous learning mindset because technology evolves very rapidly.
